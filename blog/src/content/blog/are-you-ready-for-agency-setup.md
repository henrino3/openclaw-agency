# Are You Ready for Done-For-You Agent Setup?

You've read about AI agents. You get the potential. You know your business could use automation. The question is: do you build it yourself, or pay someone to do it?

The "obvious" answer is DIY. You're smart. You can figure it out. Plus, paying someone thousands for a setup feels steep when there are YouTube tutorials.

But here's what actually happens sometimes: You spend weeks learning Docker, OAuth flows, and skill configuration. You get partway there. Then a platform update breaks something and you don't know how to fix it. The agent sits unused, and you're back to doing everything manually.

Agency setup isn't for everyone. But when it's right, it's the difference between "I'll get to that eventually" and "this is working tomorrow."

## Signs You're Ready

**You're drowning in repetitive work**

If you're still validating product-market fit or testing ideas, you probably don't need an agent yet. You need focus.

But if you're doing the same email triage, data entry, customer follow-ups, or reporting every week - and it's eating time you should spend on strategy - that's the signal.

Agency setup makes sense when you know what you need automated. Vague "I want AI to help my business" isn't ready. "I need these five workflows handled so I can focus on sales" is.

**You've tried DIY and hit a wall**

Some people underestimate the complexity. Others overestimate their patience for debugging authentication flows.

If you've spent 10+ hours trying to get something working and you're stuck, that's data. You now know this isn't your highest-value use of time.

**Downtime costs you money**

If your agent breaks and you can't fix it fast, what happens? For hobbyists: minor inconvenience. For businesses: lost revenue, missed opportunities, customer frustration.

Agency setups come with support and maintenance. When something breaks, you're not alone with GitHub issues and Reddit threads.

**You value predictability over control**

DIY gives you total control. Agency gives you predictability.

If you want to tinker, customize every detail, and own the whole stack, build it yourself. That's a valid choice.

If you want a system that works next week, gets maintained when platforms change, and doesn't require you to become an infrastructure expert, agency makes sense.

## Signs You're NOT Ready

**You don't know what you'd automate yet**

Agents solve known problems. If you can't describe your workflows clearly, you're not ready for automation. Spend time documenting what you actually do first.

**You're pre-revenue or in heavy pivot mode**

Your workflows will change too much. Build manually until things stabilize, then automate.

**You're excited by the building process**

If you genuinely want to learn agent architecture, enjoy debugging, and have the time - DIY is the better path. You'll learn more and own the result.

**Your budget is tight and you have time**

If your opportunity cost isn't high yet, or if you're bootstrapping hard, DIY first. Agency setup is for when your time is expensive.

## What Agency Setup Actually Delivers

**Scoping:** You explain your workflows, they translate into agent tasks. Good agencies push back on over-complicated setups.

**Custom configuration:** Not "here's a template," but "here's your agent, configured for your tools, your auth, your workflows."

**Testing and handoff:** They make sure it works before you touch it. Then they train you on how to use it (not how to build it).

**Maintenance window:** When platforms change or something breaks, you have support. You're not stuck.

**Documentation:** Clear runbooks for common tasks, troubleshooting, and small changes.

## What It Doesn't Deliver

**Infinite customization:** Agencies build what you need, not every edge case you might someday want. That's a feature, not a bug. Scope control keeps it working.

**Teaching you to code:** If you want to learn agent development, take a course. Agency setup is for people who want results, not education.

**Ongoing strategy:** They set up the system. You decide what to automate next.

## The Real Question

"If I spend the next 40 hours learning agent setup, debugging OAuth, and configuring skills - is that my highest-value use of time?"

If yes: DIY is probably the right path. You'll learn a lot and own the result.

If no: Agency setup gets you to "working system" in days instead of months, and lets you focus on what you're actually good at.

Both are valid. Pick the one that matches where you are.

## How to Evaluate an Agency

Not all done-for-you services are equal. Here's what to look for:

**They ask hard questions upfront:** If an agency says yes to everything without pushing back, they're either new or overselling.

**They show you past work:** Not "we've done hundreds of setups" but "here's what we built for a client in your industry."

**Clear deliverables:** What exactly are you getting? Vague "full agent setup" isn't enough.

**Support terms:** What happens when something breaks? Is it covered? For how long? What's out of scope?

**Handoff process:** Do they train you, or just drop off a system and disappear?

## When to Pull the Trigger

You're ready for agency setup when:
1. You can clearly describe what you want automated
2. The time saved is worth more than the cost
3. You'd rather spend your hours on business problems than infrastructure problems
4. You need it working soon, not eventually

You're NOT ready when:
1. You're still figuring out what your workflows even are
2. You want to learn agent development (valid goal, different path)
3. Your business is in heavy flux
4. You have more time than money right now

Pick the option that matches where you are, not where you think you should be.
